# One big room

The concept is a single daylit volume under a **5/8-sphere geodesic** shell.
No hallways, no spare rooms — you cook, sleep, bathe, move, and grow plants in
one place, and every function shares the same light.

Here it is, whole — an AI-generated concept (Grok Imagine) that captures the
vibe better than any of the rough 3D studies can: a rope net loft under the
oculus, gymnastic rings, a freestanding tub, plants along the glazed band, and
wall bars, all in one room.

Underneath that dream is real geometry. It starts as a bare, translucent shell.

<Image src={bare} alt="Bare translucent geodesic shell" sizes="(min-width: 768px) 44rem, 100vw" class="rounded-panel border border-line" />

It starts as a bare, translucent shell — a **5/8 sphere**, taller than a
hemisphere so the walls stand up before they curve overhead. That extra
eighth is what makes the interior livable instead of a shallow igloo.

## Building the room, one layer at a time

The design wasn't drawn all at once. It was grown in Blender, one honest layer
at a time: a floor, then a kitchen, then furniture, then the things that make a
dome feel like *this* dome.

<Image src={kitchen} alt="Perimeter kitchen counter and floating shelves" sizes="(min-width: 768px) 44rem, 100vw" class="rounded-panel border border-line" />

The **kitchen hugs the perimeter** — a curved counter and floating shelves that
follow the shell instead of fighting it. Curved walls are famously hard to
furnish; the answer is to stop bringing in rectangles and let the built-ins
trace the curve.

<Image src={rings} alt="Gymnastic rings hung from the apex" sizes="(min-width: 768px) 44rem, 100vw" class="rounded-panel border border-line" />

From the apex hang **gymnastic rings**. The open center of a dome is its best
feature — so the design keeps it clear and hangs movement from the top: rings,
a climbing-net loft, wall bars around the edge. The room *is* the gym.

<Image src={plants} alt="Biophilic planting along the glazed band" sizes="(min-width: 768px) 44rem, 100vw" class="rounded-panel border border-line" />

Then **plants** — along the glazed band where the daylight lands. This is the
biophilic heart of the idea: a warm, living, one-room interior, more terrarium
than house.

## The geometry

<Image src={geometry} alt="Corrected 5/8-sphere geodesic geometry study" sizes="(min-width: 768px) 44rem, 100vw" class="rounded-panel border border-line" />

Underneath the mood is real geometry — a corrected **5/8-sphere geodesic**
frequency study. Getting the sphere fraction right is what gives the room its
usable wall height and its central oculus.
